backstory.

I've always been a big fan of recounting the many stories I've shared with my friends â€” namely, the inside jokes that have developed over the years. So I decided to create "Remember When" as a place for my friends and I to bring up our inside jokes.

Incidentally, it was also a great way to practice building and maintaining a Rails app.

Design Process.

My initial design for "Remember When" involved only three components. The first was a stream of posts, or "memories", with the top-ranked post highlighted at the very top of the stream. The second component was the login/signup forms, which would sit on the top of the page. Finally, there was the user dashboard, which would allow the user to create more posts, and check their score. Furthermore, the entire site's contents would be public, so every post could be seen.

However, as I kept thinking over the potential use cases, it became apparent to me that many people would most likely prefer a personalized dashboard, instead of a fully public list of posts from random users. As well, considering how most people would prefer to share posts with friends rather than create public posts, I decided to add the ability to create private groups, where people could create their own memory stream and share them width others using a custom link, as well as the post ability to share to their private stream, or to the general public.
